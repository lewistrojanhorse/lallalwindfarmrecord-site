Endorsed compliance methodology

Noise Compliance Testing Plan

The approved Noise Compliance Testing Plan is the bridge between the Planning Permit, NZS 6808:2010 and the post-construction noise compliance record.

Plain English

Why the NCTP matters

The NCTP was approved as part of the permit framework. It records how operational wind farm noise was to be measured, assessed and reported. For this repository, the NCTP is read as the practical method by which the planning permit and NZS 6808 framework were to be applied.

Requirement

The NCTP identifies locations, data requirements, operational measurement rules, special audible characteristic procedures and reporting steps.

Record

Later technical reports and agency records are read against the NCTP to ask whether the required pathway can be followed from the disclosed documents.

Verification

The NCTP is central to the Documentary Verification method because it connects mandatory requirements to the source record.

Framework position

Where the NCTP sits in the governing hierarchy

The NCTP does not sit alone. It is part of the same pathway used across this repository.

Mandatory pathway

NCTP requirements highlighted

Where the approved NCTP uses mandatory language, this repository preserves that language and asks whether the later disclosed record enables independent reconciliation.

AreaMandatory pathwayRepository issue
K15aaThe NCTP records the need for an updated K15aa background report before operation.Background sound, receiver-group methodology and agency custody.
Measurement locationsOperational measurements are tied to preferred receiver locations, subject to access and the approved methodology.Whether the later record can be traced back to the required locations and reference methodology.
Measurement positionMeasurements are read against receiver / dwelling proximity requirements and continuity with background monitoring locations.Continuity between pre-construction monitoring and operational assessment.
Acoustic and wind dataThe NCTP specifies the acoustic and synchronised wind data needed for assessment.Whether the disclosed record allows the compliance dataset to be independently followed.
Special Audible CharacteristicsThe NCTP includes procedures for identifying and assessing SAC.Attended observations, monitoring periods, reporting and evidentiary completeness.
ReportingReporting requirements form part of the approved documentary pathway.What was reported, when it was reported, and what agency custody record exists.
How to read the evidence

The NCTP is used as a verification map

The repository does not use the NCTP to make its own compliance finding. It uses the NCTP to identify the required steps and then asks whether those steps can be followed in the disclosed record.

K

Background report requirement, reference-location pathway and custody record.
