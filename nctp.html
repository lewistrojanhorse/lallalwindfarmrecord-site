Noise Compliance Testing Plan

The approved Noise Compliance Testing Plan (NCTP) is the bridge between the Planning Permit, NZS 6808:2010 and the post-construction noise compliance record.

Plain English

Why the NCTP matters

The NCTP was approved as part of the permit framework. Its mandatory language matters because it records how operational noise compliance was to be measured, assessed and reported.

Fact

The NCTP specifies operational noise limits, monitoring procedures, special audible characteristic procedures and reporting requirements.

Why it matters

The NCTP is not merely background commentary. It is the approved testing methodology that later compliance material is read against.

Evidence

The detailed record is in the NCTP, SRAM, Annex A, Annex B and the Documentary Verification Schedule.

Mandatory requirements highlighted

Where the approved NCTP uses shall, this repository preserves that mandatory language.
