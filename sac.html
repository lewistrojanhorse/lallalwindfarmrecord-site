Special Audible Characteristics

What SAC means, why attended observations matter, and how Annex B and the Verification Schedule treat the record.

Special Audible Characteristics

SAC is about the character of the sound, not only its level

Special Audible Characteristics are important because wind farm noise may be more noticeable where identifiable characteristics such as tonality, impulsiveness or modulation are present. The record examines how SAC was identified, observed and reported.

A

Attended observations

Human attended listening observations are relevant to identifying where SAC may be present.

R

Reporting

The NCTP reporting pathway required details of attended observations used for identifying potential SAC.

B

Annex B

Annex B and Version 1.1 record the SAC-01 documentary pathway and supplementary observations.

SAC documentary questions

QuestionWhy it matters
Were attended observations conducted?Attended listening assists in identifying whether SAC is potentially present.
When were observations conducted?Timing determines whether observations correspond with the monitoring period relied upon.
Were observations reported?The reporting record allows an independent reader to trace the basis for SAC identification or non-identification.
How does Stage 1 compare with Stage 2?Annex B records differences in how the disclosed record presents SAC observations across assessment stages.
Verification connection

SAC is a mandatory-reporting issue

The Documentary Verification Schedule treats SAC assessment as part of the NCTP mandatory reporting and assessment pathway. The purpose is not to determine whether SAC exists; it is to identify what the disclosed record permits an independent reader to verify.
