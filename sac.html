Special Audible Characteristics

Attended listening observations, objective assessment requirements and the Stage 1 and Stage 2 documentary chronology for the Lal Lal Wind Farm record.

It applies the record method to one issue: the relationship between the approved Noise Compliance Testing Plan, Special Audible Characteristics, attended listening observations, disclosed appendices and the documentary chronology.

Plain English

What SAC means

Special Audible Characteristics, often shortened to SACs, are distinctive features of wind farm sound, such as tonality, impulsiveness or modulation, that may make a sound more noticeable or intrusive than ordinary broadband sound.

This page does not determine whether SACs were or were not present. It explains the approved observation procedure and reproduces the documentary record showing how attended listening observations were reported.

Fact

The approved NCTP required attended observations by a qualified acoustic engineer experienced in wind farm sound.

Why it matters

SAC identification was not only a meter-based exercise. The NCTP required listening observations and reporting of those observations.

Evidence

The key record is found in the approved NCTP, Stage 1 Elaine report, Stage 2 report and disclosed attended-observation appendices.

Approved methodology

What the approved NCTP required

The endorsed Noise Compliance Testing Plan identifies when attended listening observations are required, where they are to be undertaken and how they form part of the mandatory methodology for assessing Special Audible Characteristics.

The approved Noise Compliance Test Plan set out how SACs were to be identified and reported. The extracts below are included so readers can compare the approved procedure with the observations reproduced in later compliance reports.

  • SAC identification based on attended observations.
  • Observations by a qualified acoustic engineer experienced in wind farm sound.
  • Three sets of attended observations during each stage of monitoring.
  • Wind farm operating at the time of observations.
  • At least one set of attended observations during the night.
  • Informative observations near turbines during at least one set.
  • Near-turbine observations typically 100 m to 300 m from nearest turbines.
  • Compliance reports to include full details of attended observations and supplementary procedures.

Information session context

During the public information session, SLR described attended listening as a process of listening to the wind farm at different times of the day and in different conditions, and described that process as a gateway to further objective analysis where a particular sound character was heard.

The same information session also stated that some surveys were conducted but were not documented in the report because, after the fact, SLR determined that the wind farm was not producing electricity and elected not to count those surveys. This page places that explanation alongside the Appendix D records reproduced in the Stage 1 report.

Public explanation

SLR explanation of attended observation timing

Public Information Session

Q&A – Part B of Question 1 (commencing at 52:40)

During the SLR Public Information Session, SLR responded to a question concerning the attended observations undertaken on 10 March 2021 and 31 March 2021 and their relationship to the AEMO operational record.

  • SLR explained that the 10 March 2021 attended observations occurred during a period of low cut-in wind speed, with contemporaneous survey notes recording slow turbine rotation and a faint audible hum.
  • SLR stated that the 31 March 2021 attended observations also occurred during a low wind speed period.
  • SLR explained there was likely a one-hour discrepancy between the survey times and the AEMO operational data because the survey was undertaken during Australian Daylight Saving Time, whereas the AEMO data was believed to be displayed in Australian Eastern Standard Time.
  • SLR stated that it was likely the turbines were operating, albeit at a low level, during the relevant attended observations.
Record note: This explanation should be read together with the record's analytical reconstruction and the underlying documentary material. It provides SLR's public explanation of the timing relationship discussed during the information session.

Why attended observations matter

Listening forms part of the methodology

Unlike sound level measurements alone, the assessment of Special Audible Characteristics requires attended observations because some sound characteristics are initially identified through listening before any further objective assessment is undertaken where necessary.
