Governing Framework

Regulatory Framework

The Framework identifies the planning, technical and regulatory obligations used to answer the central question examined throughout this website: do the available documents show that the Lal Lal Wind Farm's planning, technical and regulatory obligations are being met?

Purpose of this page: This page identifies the documents that create or define the obligations examined throughout the website. It provides the starting point for comparing what was required with the evidence found, the documentary gaps identified, the questions raised and the responses received.

Governing hierarchy

Each layer has a different documentary role

Not every document proves the same thing. Some create obligations, some prescribe methods, some record assessment work, and others show audit, regulatory or Freedom of Information responses. The role of each document must be kept distinct when examining whether the available record demonstrates compliance.

Planning law
The Planning & Environment Act provides the statutory planning context within which the project permit operates.
Law
Planning Permit
The Planning Permit is the operative planning instrument. It establishes the project permission and the relevant conditions.
Permit
Permit Conditions 23–25
Conditions 23–25 connect the permit framework to the technical noise assessment pathway.
Conditions
NZS 6808:2010
NZS 6808:2010 supplies technical language and wind farm sound assessment concepts used by later documents.
Standard
Noise Compliance Testing Plan
The Noise Compliance Testing Plan sets out the endorsed compliance monitoring and reporting methodology.
NCTP
Assessment and reporting record
The post-construction assessment record is where the endorsed pathway should be applied and documented, including issues such as background sound and Special Audible Characteristics.
Assessment
Environmental auditor material
Environmental auditor material forms part of the later regulatory and verification record.
Audit

Documentary method

How each issue is examined

The website applies one consistent method: identify what was required, identify the evidence found, record any documentary gap, publish the question raised, publish the response received and state the current documentary position.

1

Requirement

Identify the governing condition, method or reporting obligation.

2

Evidence found

Identify the documents, reports, correspondence and disclosures located in the available record that address the requirement.

3

Documentary gap

Identify any required step, supporting document, inconsistency or explanation that has not been located in the available record.

4

Question raised

Record the question put to the operator, consultant, regulator or other organisation.

5

Response received

Publish the answer received and record where no response, or no substantive answer to the question, was provided.

6

Current documentary position

State whether the available documents now show how the obligation was addressed, without substituting a regulatory finding for the documentary record.

Important: This website does not make regulatory findings. It asks whether the available documents show that the relevant obligations are being met. It identifies what was required, what evidence was found, what documentary gaps were identified, what questions were raised and what responses were received.
Issue-page model

The same method is used throughout the website

K15aa provides the clearest worked example. Each issue page keeps the requirement, evidence found, documentary gap, questions raised, responses received and current documentary position separate so readers can follow the documentary pathway without changing the test from page to page.

Why this matters

Using the same method across the website lets readers compare K15aa, NCTP, NZS 6808, SAC, permit amendment and FOI issues against the same central question: whether the available documents show that the relevant obligations are being met.

Agency Records

It allows readers to see what each authority was asked, what documents or decisions were provided, what position was stated and what remained unanswered or not located.
