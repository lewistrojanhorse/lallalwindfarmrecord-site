Governing hierarchy

Each layer has a different role

The framework matters because the source documents do not all do the same thing. Some create obligations. Some define technical methods. Some record assessment. Some disclose what agencies hold or rely upon.

Planning law
The Planning & Environment Act provides the statutory planning context within which the project permit operates.
Law
Planning Permit
The Planning Permit is the operative planning instrument. It establishes the project permission and the relevant conditions.
Permit
Permit Conditions 23–25
Conditions 23–25 connect the permit framework to the technical noise assessment pathway.
Conditions
NZS 6808:2010
NZS 6808:2010 supplies technical language and wind farm sound assessment concepts used by later documents.
Standard
Noise Compliance Testing Plan
The Noise Compliance Testing Plan sets out the endorsed compliance monitoring and reporting methodology.
NCTP
Assessment and reporting record
The post-construction assessment record is where the endorsed pathway is applied, including issues such as background sound and Special Audible Characteristics.
Assessment
Environmental auditor material
Environmental auditor material forms part of the later regulatory and verification record.
Audit
Agency and FOI record
Regulator correspondence and Freedom of Information decisions identify what has been disclosed, relied upon, or not located within the administrative record.

Understanding the Record

How evidence is read in this repository

The repository does not ask the reader to accept a conclusion first. It asks the reader to follow the documents.

1

Requirement

Identify the governing requirement, condition, technical method or reporting obligation.

2

Documentary record

Identify the disclosed documents that show how the requirement was addressed.

3

Verification question

Ask whether an independent reader can reconcile the requirement with the disclosed record.

Important: This repository does not determine compliance, breach, liability, legal rights or regulatory findings. It records the documentary pathway and whether the disclosed record enables independent reconciliation.
Benchmark presentation

K15aa remains the evidence-page model

The K15aa page is the benchmark for future evidentiary presentation. It separates the requirement, the disclosed record, the administrative observation and the source documents.

Why this matters

Using the same method across the repository prevents each issue from becoming a separate argument. It lets readers compare K15aa, NCTP, NZS 6808, SAC, permit amendment and FOI issues against one consistent framework.
