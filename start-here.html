Plain-English Guide

Start Here

This page explains the repository for readers who are new to wind farm regulation, acoustics, planning permits and Freedom of Information records.

Purpose of this guide: This page does not replace the documentary record. It explains how to read the record so that a high-school student, local resident, journalist, investigator or technically experienced reader can follow the same pathway back to the source documents.
Ten plain-English questions

What am I looking at?

The repository is organised so readers can begin with simple explanations and then move into the formal record.

1

What is this website?

A public administrative repository preserving documents, chronology, FOI material and verification work concerning the Lal Lal Wind Farm.

2

What is the record about?

The record concerns the planning and technical framework for wind farm noise compliance, including how mandatory requirements were documented.

3

What is a planning permit?

A planning permit is the legal instrument that sets conditions for how a project may be built and operated.

4

What is NZS 6808?

NZS 6808:2010 is the technical standard incorporated into the planning framework for assessing wind farm noise.

5

What is the NCTP?

The Noise Compliance Testing Plan is the endorsed testing methodology used to assess operational wind farm noise against the planning framework.

6

What is K15aa?

K15aa is a reference location in the documentary record for the southern noise-sensitive receiver group associated with the Elaine section of the wind farm.

7

What is background sound?

Background sound is the baseline sound level used to help determine operational limits. The Standard defines it as measured before wind turbines are installed.

8

What is SAC?

Special Audible Characteristics are identifiable sound features such as tonality or amplitude modulation that require specific assessment under the framework.

9

What is FOI?

Freedom of Information material helps show what documents were held, released, not located, or relied upon by agencies at different points in the chronology.

10

Where is the evidence?

Each issue page points back to the primary documents, including the Statement, Crystallisation Notice, SRAMs, Annexes, FOI decisions and technical records.

Two definitions to understand first

Background sound

The A-frequency-weighted L90 centile level measured prior to the installation of any wind turbines in an area.

Plain English: the background sound record is meant to describe the sound environment before wind turbines are installed. That is why the chronology of background monitoring matters.

Notional boundary

A line 20 metres from any side of a dwelling or other building used for a noise sensitive activity, or the legal boundary where this is closer to such a building.

Plain English: this definition helps identify where noise limits are assessed for a noise-sensitive location.

Why K15aa is not just one-house issue

K15aa should be read as a reference location within the endorsed testing framework. The repository presents K15aa in relation to the southern noise-sensitive receiver group associated with the Elaine section of the Lal Lal Wind Farm.

Individual receiver references, including J14aa, are included where they appear in primary source documents or are necessary to explain how the reference methodology was applied.

How to read this repository

Read the plain-English explanation first, then open the linked primary documents. Where a page identifies a statutory requirement, technical definition, FOI outcome or administrative observation, it should be understood by reference to the underlying source documents.
