Orientation

What and where is the Lal Lal Wind Farm?

The repository begins with the project itself before moving into the planning, technical and regulatory record.

The wind farm

The Lal Lal Wind Farm is located near Elaine and Yendon, south-east of Ballarat in Victoria.

The project is commonly described as a 60 turbine wind farm with an installed capacity of approximately 228 megawatts.

This website is not a general website about wind farms. It is a public administrative and evidentiary repository concerning the Lal Lal Wind Farm record.

Lal Lal Wind Farm entrance sign. Image included only as contextual orientation for readers.

Documentary record

Why this repository exists

The repository preserves and explains the continuing administrative record concerning the planning, construction, operation, monitoring, assessment and regulatory treatment of the Lal Lal Wind Farm.

The repository does not ask readers to accept conclusions. Instead, it explains the documentary record and provides direct access to the underlying source material so readers can form their own understanding.

1

To preserve the record

The site brings together planning documents, technical reports, correspondence, Freedom of Information material, Trustee records and verification work in one public location.

2

To make the record readable

Many source documents use planning, acoustic, regulatory and legal language. The website explains those terms progressively before linking readers back to the evidence.

3

To separate explanation from evidence

Plain-English pages help readers understand the pathway. Evidence pages and document links allow readers to check the underlying source material directly.

Community Reference Group record

How the documentary record developed

The repository follows the documents chronologically, including the project’s transition from construction into commissioning and later operational assessment.

Construction and commissioning

The Community Reference Group record is treated as part of the documentary chronology because it records public-facing project updates at important stages.

In that record, the project was described as having completed construction while commissioning continued.

The distinction is important because commissioning forms part of the documentary chronology used throughout this repository.

Community Reference Group minutes published on the Lal Lal Wind Farms website provide an important contemporaneous reference point within that chronology.

View Community Reference Group Minutes

Why the chronology matters

For this repository, timing is not a side issue. It helps readers understand the relationship between pre-construction monitoring, construction, commissioning, post-construction assessment and later regulatory reliance.

How one report can have different documentary roles

The June 2018 Sonus assessment is a planning-stage predictive report. EPA later identified it as a document used to assess compliance, while the formal post-construction assessment and verification reports identify the Marshall Day/NCTP pathway. The repository keeps those roles separate.
