Glossary

This page explains the technical, planning and administrative terms used throughout the Lal Lal Wind Farm Record. It is designed to let a reader understand the language of the record before moving into the evidence pages.

Start HereDocument Library
How this glossary works

One explanation, then the evidence

Each entry gives a plain-English explanation, identifies the documentary or authoritative meaning where appropriate, explains why the term matters in this repository, and points the reader back to the relevant evidence pages.

Reading rule: technical terms are explained here first. Evidence pages then use the term consistently rather than repeating the definition each time.
Core terms

Planning, technical and administrative language

The entries below follow the repository structure: plain English, documentary meaning, why it matters, referenced pages, and read more.

Lal Lal Wind Farm
Plain English: The wind farm southeast of Ballarat that is the subject of this repository.
Documentary description: The project is recorded as a wind farm operating under a planning permit framework and related technical noise compliance documents.
Why it matters: This repository is about this project record, not wind farms generally.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Framework, Chronology, Documents.
Read more: Start Here, Administrative Record.
Project
Planning Permit
Plain English: The permission framework that authorises the wind farm and sets enforceable conditions.
Documentary description: The planning permit contains conditions relevant to noise, assessment and compliance obligations.
Why it matters: The permit is the starting point for understanding why later documents such as NZS 6808, the NCTP and compliance reports matter.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Framework, NCTP, Permit Amendment.
Read more: Framework, Permit Amendment.
Framework
Conditions 23–25
Plain English: The permit conditions that connect the project to the approved noise compliance pathway.
Documentary description: These conditions are part of the planning permit framework and are relevant to noise limits, testing and compliance demonstration.
Why it matters: They are central to the documentary pathway from permit to testing plan to later assessment.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Framework, NCTP, Permit Amendment.
Read more: Framework, NCTP.
Permit
NZS 6808:2010
Plain English: The technical standard used to assess wind farm sound under the planning framework.
Documentary description: NZS 6808:2010 provides technical methods and definitions for wind farm sound assessment, including background sound and operational noise assessment concepts.
Why it matters: It supplies key technical language used in the NCTP, background monitoring and compliance assessment record.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, NZS 6808, NCTP, K15aa, SAC.
Read more: NZS 6808, NCTP.
Standard
Noise Compliance Testing Plan
Plain English: The approved testing plan that sets out how wind farm noise compliance was to be assessed.
Documentary description: The NCTP records monitoring locations, assessment methods, reporting requirements and special audible characteristic assessment requirements.
Why it matters: It is the bridge between the planning permit, NZS 6808 and the later compliance record.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, NCTP, K15aa, SAC, Verification.
Read more: NCTP, Documentary Verification.
NCTP
Background Sound
Plain English: The existing sound environment before or without the wind farm contribution being assessed.
Documentary description: Within the technical assessment framework, background sound is used to establish the relationship between existing sound levels and wind speed.
Why it matters: Background sound is central to the K15aa record, because the validity and use of baseline monitoring affect later compliance interpretation.
Referenced throughout: K15aa, NZS 6808, NCTP, Technical Reports.
Read more: K15aa, NZS 6808.
Core
K15aa
Plain English: A noise assessment location that became central to the repository because of the disclosed background sound record.
Documentary description: K15aa is addressed through the NCTP requirement, replacement monitoring, later SLR reporting and FOI disclosure record.
Why it matters: It is the benchmark evidence page for how the repository presents an issue: requirement, disclosed record, gap or reconciliation question, and source documents.
Referenced throughout: K15aa, Verification, Documents, Technical Reports.
Read more: K15aa, Documentary Verification.
Benchmark
Commissioning
Plain English: The period when completed turbines or project components are being tested and brought into operation.
Documentary description: The repository records commissioning through the CRG documentary record and later operational chronology material.
Why it matters: Commissioning timing matters because several assessment and reporting obligations depend on when construction, operation or testing milestones occurred.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Chronology, Framework, NCTP.
Read more: Start Here, Chronology.
Timeline
Special Audible Characteristics
Plain English: Particular sound features, such as tonality or other clearly noticeable characteristics, that may require separate assessment.
Documentary description: The NCTP and NZS 6808 framework require consideration of special audible characteristics through relevant assessment and reporting steps.
Why it matters: The SAC page records whether the disclosed material enables independent verification of attended observations and reporting requirements.
Referenced throughout: SAC, NCTP, NZS 6808, Verification.
Read more: SAC, NCTP.
SAC
Environmental Auditor
Plain English: An independent auditor role referred to in the regulatory and technical record.
Documentary description: Auditor material is relevant to the Noise Management Plan, regulatory reliance and later administrative record.
Why it matters: The repository asks what auditor material is disclosed and whether the record identifies the basis for regulatory reliance.
Referenced throughout: Framework, Verification, Documents, Record Status.
Read more: Documentary Verification, Documents.
Audit
Freedom of Information
Plain English: A legal process for requesting documents from public agencies.
Documentary description: FOI decisions and released documents form part of the documentary custody and verification record.
Why it matters: FOI material shows what documents agencies hold, disclose, rely on or say they cannot locate.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, FOI Register, Documents, Verification.
Read more: FOI Register, Documents.
FOI
Documentary Verification
Plain English: Checking whether the disclosed documents allow an independent reader to follow and reconcile the required steps.
Documentary description: The repository uses documentary verification to connect the planning permit, NZS 6808, NCTP, monitoring reports, auditor material, regulator correspondence and FOI record.
Why it matters: It keeps the site focused on what the record shows, rather than making findings outside the documents.
Referenced throughout: Verification, K15aa, SAC, Documents, Record Status.
Read more: Documentary Verification, Record Status.
Method
Crystallised Record
Plain English: The point at which the Trustee treats a defined body of documents and correspondence as a settled record for publication or submission purposes.
Documentary description: The home page records the administrative and evidentiary record as crystallised at 29 June 2026.
Why it matters: It tells readers what record package is being relied upon at a particular point in time.
Referenced throughout: Home, Record Status, Documents, Chronology.
Read more: Record Status, Administrative Record.
Status
Administrative Record
Plain English: The collected record of documents, correspondence, decisions, disclosures and chronology relevant to the matter.
Documentary description: This repository publishes the Trustee's administrative and evidentiary record in a structured public format.
Why it matters: It provides the foundation for all issue pages and limits the site to documentary chronology and administrative observation.
Referenced throughout: Home, Record, Documents, Chronology, Verification.
Read more: Administrative Record, Chronology.
