Repository Learning Hub

Glossary

The Glossary is the central learning hub of the Lal Lal Wind Farm Record. It explains the planning, technical and administrative terms used throughout the repository before readers move into the evidentiary pages.

Start HereDocument Library
How this glossary works

One explanation, then the evidence

Each entry gives a plain-English explanation, identifies the documentary or authoritative meaning where appropriate, explains why the term matters in this repository, and points the reader back to the relevant evidence pages.

Repository principle: Technical terms are explained once in the Glossary. Other pages link back here rather than repeating definitions. This helps maintain consistency throughout the repository.
Core terms

Planning, technical and administrative language

The entries below follow the repository structure: plain English, documentary meaning, why it matters, referenced pages, and read more.

Documentary Role
Plain English: The particular function a document performs within the record.
Why it matters: A report can be a planning-stage prediction, a later correspondence reference, an agency reliance document or part of an FOI custody record without being the technical basis of every later assessment.
Example: The June 2018 Sonus assessment is a planning-stage predictive report; EPA later identified it as a document used to assess compliance, while the formal PCNA and verification reports identify the Marshall Day/NCTP pathway.
Read more: June 2018 Sonus documentary guide.
Method
Lal Lal Wind Farm
Plain English: The wind farm south-east of Ballarat that is the subject of this repository.
Documentary or authoritative description: The project is recorded as a wind farm operating under a planning permit framework and related technical noise compliance documents.
Why it matters: This repository is about this project record, not wind farms generally.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Framework, Chronology, Documents.
Read more: Start Here, Administrative Record.
Project
Planning Permit
Plain English: The permission framework that authorises the wind farm and sets enforceable conditions.
Documentary or authoritative description: The planning permit contains conditions relevant to noise, assessment and compliance obligations.
Why it matters: The permit is the starting point for understanding why later documents such as NZS 6808, the NCTP and compliance reports matter.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Framework, NCTP, Permit Amendment.
Read more: Framework, Permit Amendment.
Framework
Conditions 23–25
Plain English: The permit conditions that connect the project to the approved noise compliance pathway.
Documentary or authoritative description: These conditions are part of the planning permit framework and are relevant to noise limits, testing and compliance demonstration.
Why it matters: They are central to the documentary pathway from permit to testing plan to later assessment.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Framework, NCTP, Permit Amendment.
Read more: Framework, NCTP.
Permit
NZS 6808:2010
Plain English: The technical standard used to assess wind farm sound under the planning framework.
Documentary or authoritative description: NZS 6808:2010 provides technical methods and definitions for wind farm sound assessment, including background sound and operational noise assessment concepts.
Why it matters: It supplies key technical language used in the NCTP, background monitoring and compliance assessment record.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, NZS 6808, NCTP, K15aa, SAC.
Read more: NZS 6808, NCTP.
Standard
Noise Compliance Testing Plan
Plain English: The approved testing plan that sets out how wind farm noise compliance was to be assessed.
Documentary or authoritative description: The NCTP records monitoring locations, assessment methods, reporting requirements and special audible characteristic assessment requirements.
Why it matters: It is the bridge between the planning permit, NZS 6808 and the later compliance record.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, NCTP, K15aa, SAC, Verification.
Read more: NCTP, Documentary Verification.
NCTP
Background Sound
Plain English: The sound environment that exists before wind turbines are constructed and begin operating. It becomes the reference against which later operational wind farm sound is assessed.
Documentary or authoritative description: Within the technical assessment framework, background sound is used to establish the relationship between existing sound levels and wind speed.
Why it matters: Background sound is central to the K15aa record, because the validity and use of baseline monitoring affect later compliance interpretation.
Referenced throughout: K15aa, NZS 6808, NCTP, Technical Reports.
Read more: K15aa, NZS 6808.
Core
K15aa
Plain English: A dwelling (house) identified as K15aa within the technical assessment documents. It became central to this repository because of the disclosed background sound record associated with that location.
Documentary or authoritative description: K15aa is addressed through the NCTP requirement, replacement monitoring, later SLR reporting and FOI disclosure record.
Why it matters: It is the benchmark evidence page for how the repository presents an issue: requirement, disclosed record, gap or reconciliation question, and source documents.
Referenced throughout: K15aa, Verification, Documents, Technical Reports.
Read more: K15aa, Documentary Verification.
Benchmark
Commissioning
Plain English: The period during which completed wind turbines or project components are progressively tested and brought into operation before the wind farm is regarded as fully commissioned.
Documentary or authoritative description: The repository records commissioning through Community Reference Group documentation and later operational chronology material, including the disclosed record identifying the commissioning of the final turbine.
Why it matters: Commissioning timing matters because several assessment, monitoring and reporting obligations depend upon when the final turbine was commissioned and the wind farm became fully operational.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Chronology, Framework, NCTP.
Read more: Start Here, ChronologyAgency Records.
Timeline
Special Audible Characteristics
Plain English: Particular sound features, such as tonality or other clearly noticeable characteristics, that may require separate assessment.
Documentary or authoritative description: The NCTP and NZS 6808 framework require consideration of special audible characteristics through relevant assessment and reporting steps.
Why it matters: The SAC page records whether the disclosed material enables independent verification of attended observations and reporting requirements.
Referenced throughout: SAC, NCTP, NZS 6808, Verification.
Read more: SAC, NCTP.
SAC
Environmental Auditor
Plain English: An independent auditor role referred to in the regulatory and technical record.
Documentary or authoritative description: Auditor material is relevant to the Noise Management Plan, regulatory reliance and later administrative record.
Why it matters: The repository asks what auditor material is disclosed and whether the record identifies the basis for regulatory reliance.
Referenced throughout: Framework, Verification, Documents, Record Status.
Read more: Documentary Verification, Documents.
Audit
Freedom of Information
Plain English: A legal process for requesting documents from public agencies.
Documentary or authoritative description: Freedom of Information decisions and the documents released through those decisions form part of the repository's documentary custody and verification record.
Why it matters: FOI material helps identify what documents public agencies hold, disclose, rely upon, or state they cannot locate, allowing the documentary record to be independently verified.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, FOI Register, Documents, Verification.
Read more: FOI Register, Documents.
FOI
Documentary Verification
Plain English: Checking whether the disclosed documents allow an independent reader to follow and reconcile the required steps.
Documentary or authoritative description: The repository uses documentary verification to connect the planning permit, NZS 6808, NCTP, monitoring reports, auditor material, regulator correspondence and FOI record.
Why it matters: It keeps the site focused on what the record shows, rather than making findings outside the documents.
Referenced throughout: Verification, K15aa, SAC, Documents, Record Status.
Read more: Documentary Verification, Record Status.
Method
Crystallised Record
Plain English: The point at which the Trustee treats a defined body of documents and correspondence as a settled record for publication or submission purposes.
Documentary or authoritative description: The home page records the administrative and evidentiary record as crystallised at 29 June 2026.
Why it matters: It tells readers what record package is being relied upon at a particular point in time.
Referenced throughout: Home, Record Status, Documents, Chronology.
Read more: Record Status, Administrative Record.

Administrative Record
Plain English: The collected record of documents, correspondence, decisions, disclosures and chronology relevant to the matter.
Documentary or authoritative description: This repository publishes the Trustee's administrative and evidentiary record in a structured public format.
Why it matters: It provides the foundation for all issue pages and limits the site to documentary chronology and administrative observation.
Referenced throughout: Home, Record, Documents, Chronology, Verification.
Read more: Administrative Record, Chronology.
Record
Notional Boundary
Plain English: An imaginary boundary extending 20 metres around a dwelling (house), used within wind farm noise assessment to determine where sound may be assessed.
Documentary or authoritative description: The concept is used within NZS 6808:2010 and the endorsed Noise Compliance Testing Plan as part of the assessment methodology for noise-sensitive locations.
Why it matters: Understanding the notional boundary helps readers understand where sound assessments relate to a dwelling and why measurement locations are described in technical reports.
Referenced throughout: NZS 6808, NCTP, K15aa.
Read more: NZS 6808, NCTP.
Term
Planning and Environment Act 1987
Plain English: The Victorian law that provides the planning framework within which planning permits and permit conditions operate.
Documentary or authoritative description: The Act is the legislative starting point for the planning pathway presented in this repository.
Why it matters: It helps readers understand where the Planning Permit sits within the broader legal framework.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Framework, Permit Amendment, Chronology.
Read more: Framework.
Term
Pre-construction Assessments
Plain English: Acoustic monitoring, prediction and assessment undertaken before wind farm operation.
Documentary or authoritative description: This includes the earlier planning-stage assessment, Marshall Day background monitoring, Sonus predictive assessments and associated Environmental Auditor peer review material.
Why it matters: It separates the predictive and background record established before operation from construction, commissioning and later post-construction compliance assessment.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Framework, Chronology, K15aa, NZS 6808 and Technical Reports.
Read more: Pre-construction Acoustic Assessments, Technical Reports.
Term
Construction
Plain English: The period during which the physical wind farm infrastructure was built.
Documentary or authoritative description: Construction is the transitional phase between pre-construction monitoring and the later operational (post-construction) assessment of the wind farm. It marks the period during which the physical infrastructure is built before commissioning begins.
Why it matters: Construction timing matters because it separates pre-construction monitoring and assessment from commissioning, post-construction monitoring and later compliance activities.
Referenced throughout: Start Here, Chronology, Technical Reports.
Read more: Chronology.
Term
Post-construction Monitoring and Assessment
Plain English: Monitoring and assessment undertaken after construction as part of evaluating operational wind farm sound.
Documentary or authoritative description: The post-construction pathway is governed by the Planning Permit, NZS 6808:2010 and the endorsed Noise Compliance Testing Plan.
Why it matters: It provides the operational assessment record against which mandatory methodology and reporting can be examined.
Referenced throughout: NCTP, SAC, Verification, Technical Reports.
Read more: NCTP, SAC.
Term
Regulators
Plain English: Public authorities or statutory bodies appearing in the administrative record.
Documentary or authoritative description: Regulator records may show custody, disclosure, administrative reliance, correspondence or decisions within the applicable statutory framework.
Why it matters: The repository distinguishes regulator records from operator, consultant and Trustee material.
Referenced throughout: Framework, FOI Register, Chronology, Record Status.
Read more: FOI Register.
Term
Noise-sensitive Location
Plain English: A dwelling (house) or other noise-sensitive place identified within technical documents by a unique receiver identifier, such as K15aa, where wind farm sound may be assessed.
Documentary or authoritative description: Noise-sensitive locations are identified within the governing technical and assessment documents.
Why it matters: The term helps readers understand that identifiers such as K15aa refer to particular dwellings or assessment locations.
Referenced throughout: K15aa, NCTP, NZS 6808, Technical Reports.
Read more: K15aa.
Term
Noise-sensitive Receiver
Plain English: A dwelling (house) identified within technical reports by a receiver code or identifier, for example K15aa, for the purpose of noise assessment and reporting.
Documentary or authoritative description: Technical reports use receiver identifiers to refer consistently to particular dwellings and assessment locations.
Why it matters: Understanding receiver identifiers prevents readers from mistaking codes such as K15aa for report or instrument numbers.
Referenced throughout: K15aa, NCTP, NZS 6808.
Read more: K15aa.
Term
Attended Listening Observations
Plain English: Observations where an acoustician attends predetermined locations identified under the Noise Compliance Testing Plan and records what can be heard during a site visit.
Documentary or authoritative description: The endorsed Noise Compliance Testing Plan identifies when attended listening observations are required, where they are to be undertaken and how they form part of the post-construction compliance assessment methodology.
Why it matters: Attended listening observations help identify whether audible characteristics are present at assessment locations specified by the Noise Compliance Testing Plan.
Referenced throughout: SAC, NCTP, Technical Reports.
Read more: SAC, NCTP.
Term
Noise Management Plan
Plain English: A document addressing the management of operational wind turbine noise under the applicable regulatory framework.
Documentary or authoritative description: The Noise Management Plan, Environmental Auditor material and EPA disclosure record form part of the later regulatory pathway.
Why it matters: It connects operational management, auditor review and regulator custody within the administrative record.
Referenced throughout: Documents, FOI Register, Technical Reports.
Read more: Documents.

Disclosed Record
Plain English: The documentary material actually disclosed or available at a particular point in time.
Documentary or authoritative description: Documentary verification is based on the material available in the disclosed record, not assumptions about material that may exist but has not been produced.
Why it matters: The term makes clear the limits of any repository observation or verification question.
Referenced throughout: Understanding the Record, Verification, FOI Register.

Pre-construction acoustic terms

Predictive assessment and peer review

These terms support the documentary pathway from planning-stage assessment to later technical reliance.

Pre-construction Acoustic Assessment
Plain English: An acoustic assessment prepared before operation to predict wind farm noise and establish relevant assessment inputs.
Documentary meaning: The Lal Lal record includes planning-stage work, Marshall Day background monitoring, Sonus predictive assessments and an AECOM Environmental Auditor peer review.
Why it matters: These documents precede the NCTP post-construction compliance pathway and are later referenced by SLR, ARUP and EPA material.
Read more: Pre-construction Acoustic Assessments.
Technical
Predictive Noise Assessment
Plain English: Computer modelling used to estimate expected wind farm noise at surrounding receiver locations before operation.
Why it matters: Predicted levels and contours may later be cited when explaining receptor selection or comparing predictions with measured operational results.
Read more: Pre-construction Acoustic Assessments.
Technical
Environmental Auditor Peer Review
Plain English: An independent review by an EPA-appointed Environmental Auditor, supported where necessary by technical specialists.
Documentary meaning: The AECOM report dated 30 April 2018 records peer review of the April 2018 Sonus pre-construction assessment.
Read more: Pre-construction Acoustic Assessments.
Verification
Predicted Noise Contour
Plain English: A mapped line joining locations predicted to experience the same modelled sound level.
Why it matters: Later ARUP correspondence refers to the Sonus 35 dB(A) contour when explaining the documentary position for J14aa and NCTP receiver selection.
Read more: Later documentary references.
Technical
Agency Record
Plain English: A structured account of the documents, correspondence, FOI decisions and stated positions associated with a particular public authority.
Documentary or authoritative description: Within this repository, an agency record is a navigation and explanatory layer. It does not replace the source documents or determine the lawfulness or correctness of an agency's conduct.
Why it matters: It allows readers to follow one authority's documentary pathway without merging the different roles of EPA Victoria, DTP, Council and the AEIC.
Read more: Government & Regulatory Agencies.
Agency
Regulatory Record
Plain English: The documents showing how regulatory requirements, reports, correspondence and agency decisions developed over time.
Why it matters: The regulatory record helps readers distinguish the governing instruments from later technical explanations, agency correspondence and FOI custody material.
Read more: Framework, Agency Records.
Record
Government & Regulatory Agencies
Plain English: The public authorities whose records form part of this repository, including EPA Victoria, DTP, Moorabool Shire Council and the Australian Energy Infrastructure Commissioner.
Why it matters: Each authority has a different role. The repository presents their records separately so that correspondence, reliance and disclosure are not confused.
Read more: Agency Records gateway.
