Understanding the Record

This page explains how every issue is examined: identify the requirement, examine the available evidence, identify any documentary gap, record the question raised, publish the response received and state the current documentary position.

Reading position: The Glossary explains the terms. The Framework identifies the obligations. This page explains how those obligations are tested against the documentary record.
Purpose

Why the method matters

The website is organised so an independent reader can begin with the obligation, follow the available evidence and see what remains unexplained.

1

Begin with the question

Start Here introduces the central question: do the available documents show that the wind farm's obligations are being met?

2

Identify the rule

The Glossary explains the terms and the Framework identifies the planning, technical and regulatory obligations.

3

Follow the evidence

Issue pages such as K15aa and SAC apply the same method to the documents, gaps, questions and responses relevant to each issue.

Locked methodology

How each issue page should be read

Every issue page follows the same six-step sequence so readers can compare different issues without changing the method.

1

Requirement

Identify the governing condition, method or reporting obligation.

2

Available evidence

Identify the documents, reports, correspondence and disclosures that address the requirement.

3

Documentary gap

Identify what remains missing, inconsistent or unexplained.

4

Question raised

Record the question put to the operator, consultant, regulator or other organisation.

5

Response received

Publish the answer received, including where no substantive answer was provided.

6

Current documentary position

State whether the available record now explains how the obligation was addressed.

Important: This website does not make regulatory findings. It identifies what was required, examines the available evidence, identifies any documentary gaps and publishes the responses received.
Verification

Follow the evidence

The website follows the documentary record wherever it leads. It relies on primary documents, dated correspondence, disclosed records and formal decisions, and it identifies where those materials do not answer the question.

What counts as useful record material?

Useful material includes planning permits, endorsed plans, technical reports, monitoring documents, agency correspondence, public meeting records, FOI decisions, disclosed emails, auditor material and Trustee records.

Where the record is incomplete, the website identifies the gap in restrained terms rather than filling it with speculation.

What the site avoids

The website does not substitute its own findings for a regulator, court, auditor or technical decision-maker. Its function is to show what was required, what the evidence shows, what remains unexplained and how the issue was answered when raised.

FOI and agency records

Why Freedom of Information matters

Freedom of Information material helps show what agencies disclosed, what they relied upon, what they said they could not locate and what remains unexplained.

Disclosed does not always mean complete

A disclosed record is the material available for examination at a particular point in time. It may include released documents, partial releases, refusal reasons, searches, correspondence and agency decision letters.

How FOI material is used

FOI material is used to test whether the documentary pathway can be followed from the planning framework through technical assessment and regulatory reliance. It can also show where an agency response does not identify the document needed to explain a gap.
