Documentary timeline

Chronology

This chronology places the governing framework, technical assessments, commissioning material, regulatory disclosures and Trustee administrative record in date order. Its purpose is to show how the documentary pathway developed and where later material must be read against earlier requirements.

Reading position: The chronology records what particular documents say and when they arose. Where records use different dates or describe different operational milestones, the difference is preserved rather than resolved by assumption.
Purpose

Why chronology matters

A technical or regulatory document cannot be understood only by its publication date. The relevant sequence may include the date of the governing requirement, the monitoring period, construction or energisation, commissioning, report preparation, auditor review, regulatory reliance and later disclosure.

1

Requirement date

Identify when the governing permit condition, standard or endorsed methodology became part of the applicable documentary framework.

2

Event and evidence date

Distinguish the date of monitoring, construction, energisation, commissioning or assessment from the later date on which a report was issued.

3

Reliance and disclosure date

Identify when an auditor or agency referred to, relied upon, released or stated that it could not locate particular material.

How to read this page

Dates are attributed to their source

The chronology is a documentary navigation tool, not a substitute for the source documents. It separates established dates from dates reported by particular parties and identifies conflicting or incomplete chronology where the available record does not establish one reconciled position.

What an entry establishes

An entry establishes that the identified document, event, statement or disclosure appears in the available record at the stated date. It does not give every document the same authority or convert a later statement into an amendment of an earlier requirement.

What a difference in dates means

Different dates may refer to construction completion, first energisation, generation, individual turbine commissioning, final turbine commissioning, commencement of operation or administrative closure. The chronology preserves those distinctions unless a source expressly reconciles them.

Documentary hierarchy

The record developed in layers

The sequence begins with the legal and planning framework, continues through the endorsed technical method and assessment record, and then moves to auditor material, agency reliance, Freedom of Information disclosure and the continuing administrative record.

Evidentiary limits

What this chronology does not determine

The chronology does not make findings of compliance, non-compliance, causation, liability or regulatory validity. It does not infer that a missing document never existed, or that a disclosed document was the only material held or considered. It identifies the available sequence, attributes statements to their sources and directs readers to the underlying evidence.

Document date is not always event date

A report may be prepared months or years after the monitoring or event it describes. The chronology therefore preserves monitoring periods, issue dates, revision dates and disclosure dates separately.

Later reliance does not amend the framework

An agency’s later reliance upon a document records an administrative position. It does not by itself amend the planning permit, NZS 6808:2010 or the endorsed NCTP.
