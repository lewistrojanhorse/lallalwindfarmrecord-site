Chronology

The chronology page explains how the planning framework, technical record, Freedom of Information record and Trustee administrative record developed over time.

Repository context

Why chronology matters

Documents are best understood in the order they were created. Earlier documents establish the governing framework and assessment methodology, while later documents record how those requirements were applied, reviewed and disclosed. Reading the chronology helps readers understand the documentary pathway rather than viewing individual documents in isolation.

How to read this chronology

Chronology connects the evidence

This page is not a standalone finding. It is a navigation aid that places the source documents in sequence so readers can understand when each part of the disclosed record arose and how later reliance connects back to earlier requirements.

1

Framework first

The chronology begins with the Planning Permit, NZS 6808:2010 and the Noise Compliance Testing Plan.

2

Technical sequence

Technical reports are read in order: pre-construction assessments, construction and commissioning, post-construction compliance assessments, auditor verification and later review.

3

Disclosure and custody

FOI decisions and agency correspondence show what documents were disclosed, held, not located or later relied upon.

Repository pathway

The record developed in layers

The chronology follows the same governing and documentary pathway used across the repository, from the planning framework and endorsed methodology through pre-construction assessment, construction, commissioning, post-construction assessment, auditor verification and documentary disclosure.

Chronology by pathway

Three ways to follow the record

Different readers may enter the chronology from different directions. These pathways keep the timeline connected to the evidence pages.

T

Technical chronology

Follow the sequence from original background monitoring through supplementary baseline monitoring, auditor material and Appendix H.

F

FOI custody chronology

Follow the agency disclosure record and understand what documents were released, withheld or not located.

R

Trustee record chronology

Follow how the Statement, SRAMs, Annexes and Supplementary Public Interest Submission were assembled.

Evidentiary limit

What this chronology does not do

The chronology does not determine compliance, non-compliance, causation, liability or regulatory findings. It identifies the sequence of documents and administrative events so an independent reader can move from timing to source material.
