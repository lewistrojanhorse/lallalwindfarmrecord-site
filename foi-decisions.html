Freedom of Information

Understanding the documentary record

The Freedom of Information (FOI) record forms an important component of the Trustee's administrative and evidentiary record.

What this page records

Throughout the development of the administrative record, FOI requests were used to identify documents held by public authorities, understand the documentary basis of regulatory decision-making, and determine where significant documents were disclosed, not held, or could not be located.

How to read this page

The FOI record does not replace the Supporting Record and Analytical Memoranda. It provides readers with access to the original administrative decisions explaining what agencies searched for and what records were identified.

Purpose

Why FOI requests were made

The FOI program was undertaken to identify documents relied upon by regulators, establish documentary custody, and preserve the public administrative history supporting the Trustee's record.

Documents held

To identify documents held by regulators and determine which records formed part of agency files.

Documentary custody

To establish custody of significant documents, including technical reports, compliance records and regulatory correspondence.

Non-location outcomes

To record where important documents were not held, not located, or confirmed as outside the agency's records.

Administrative basis

To understand the documentary basis of regulatory decisions and administrative reliance positions.

Chronology

To preserve the chronology of document discovery and statutory disclosure over time.

Verification

To support comparison between mandatory documentary requirements and documents identified within the disclosed record.

Two phases

Two phases of documentary investigation

The FOI record is divided into two distinct phases reflecting the development of the administrative record.

Phase One — Historical Beneficiary Requests (2023–2025)

Before establishment of the Trustee's formal administrative record, the Beneficiary undertook a number of Freedom of Information requests in a private capacity.

These requests resulted in the release of significant technical reports, planning records and regulatory documents that later became part of the evidentiary foundation of the Trustee's administrative record.

Examples include the Noise Management Plan, Marshall Day Background Noise Monitoring Report, EPA decision records, Departmental planning records and Council planning records.

These requests provided important historical context but were undertaken independently of the Trustee's later administrative investigation.

Phase Two — Trustee Administrative Requests (2026)

Following establishment of the Trustee's administrative record, LEWIS TROJAN HORSE PTY LTD as Trustee undertook a structured series of Freedom of Information requests directed to relevant public authorities.

These requests were designed to verify documentary custody, identify records relied upon during regulatory decision-making, and establish whether significant documents existed within agency files.

The Trustee FOI program forms part of the completed administrative and evidentiary record submitted to the Victorian Ombudsman.

Administrative purpose

What the Trustee FOI program records

The Trustee's FOI program was not undertaken to obtain private remedies. Its purpose was to assist in documenting the administrative record.

  • documents held by regulators;
  • documents disclosed under Freedom of Information;
  • documents confirmed not to exist, not held, or not located;
  • documentary searches undertaken by agencies; and
  • the evidentiary basis of regulatory administration.
Publication note

Published FOI material

Published FOI material has been reviewed prior to publication. Where necessary, limited redactions have been made to remove personal contact information or other private information not relevant to understanding the administrative record. No substantive administrative content has been altered.
