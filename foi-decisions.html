Record context

Why Freedom of Information matters

Freedom of Information provides a lawful process for obtaining documents held by public agencies. Within this record, FOI decisions help identify what documentary material agencies hold, disclose, rely upon, or state they cannot locate.

Freedom of Information

Understanding the documentary custody record

The Freedom of Information record forms an important component of the Trustee's administrative and evidentiary record. This page identifies the FOI decisions presently published in the record and explains, in plain English, the purpose and outcome of each decision.

Publication note: Published FOI material has been reviewed prior to publication. Where necessary, limited redactions have been made to remove personal contact information or other private information not relevant to understanding the administrative record. No substantive administrative content has been altered.
How to read this page

FOI decisions are custody records, not findings

The FOI register helps readers see what documents public authorities identified, released, withheld, or said they could not locate. It is read alongside the Framework, Documentary Verification, Documents and issue pages.

Context

How the FOI record should be read

The FOI decisions do not themselves determine compliance, breach, liability or legal rights. They record the documents public authorities identified, released, withheld, or confirmed were not located following statutory disclosure processes.

The detailed analysis of the FOI material remains in the Supporting Record and Analytical Memoranda and relevant Annexes. This page provides a public gateway to the decision letters and a concise explanation of their administrative role.
Record principle

Documentary provenance

Each Freedom of Information decision forms part of the record's documentary custody record. Together, the decisions and released documents help establish the provenance of material relied upon throughout the record.
