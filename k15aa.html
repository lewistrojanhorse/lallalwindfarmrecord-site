Key documentary issue

K15aa Background Sound Record

Why the K15aa record matters, what the NCTP required, and how later documents rely upon or examine that record.

Why K15aa matters

The background sound record underpinning later assessment

K15aa is central because the NCTP identified it as a preferred noise sensitive receiver location and because later material identifies it as the relevant reference location for J14aa / 308 Elaine-Mount Mercer Road.

The simple version

Before operational wind farm noise can be assessed, the background sound environment must be understood. The NCTP records that the original K15aa monitoring was not considered suitable for representing typical background noise levels and that repeat monitoring was undertaken. It further records that an updated background noise report for K15aa was to be submitted before operation.

Why readers should care

If K15aa forms part of the background sound pathway for J14aa, then the documents concerning K15aa become essential to understanding the later operational compliance record, the post-construction assessments, and the disclosed agency custody record.

Verification question

What an independent reader is asked to reconcile

The Documentary Verification Schedule asks whether the disclosed record enables an independent reader to reconcile the governing background sound definition, the NCTP requirement for an updated K15aa report, the timing of replacement monitoring, the later reliance documents, and the disclosed record concerning submission, receipt or custody of the updated report.

1

NCTP requirement

Updated K15aa background report to be submitted prior to commencement of operation.

2

Later reliance

Subsequent documents describe, rely upon or re-analyse the replacement K15aa monitoring.

3

Disclosure record

FOI and agency custody material is used to identify what is and is not located within the disclosed administrative record.
