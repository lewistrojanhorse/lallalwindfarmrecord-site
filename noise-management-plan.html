Noise Management Plan

A source-led guide to the available October 2022 Noise Management Plan, its relationship with the endorsed Noise Compliance Testing Plan, EPA documentary custody, auditor verification correspondence, Freedom of Information releases and the later planning permit amendment pathway.

Open October 2022 NMPOpen endorsed NCTP guide
Reading position: This page distinguishes the governing framework, the NMP's technical content, documentary custody and later administrative reliance. It does not determine compliance, validity, breach or legal effect.
Document identity

The available October 2022 document

The document released through the EPA Freedom of Information record is titled Lal Lal Wind Farm — Noise Management Plan, Project No. 0467385, Version 3.0, dated 18 October 2022. Its document history records Draft, Final 01, Final 02 and Final 03 revisions.

Document details and revision history

The extract records Version 3.0 and Final Revision 03 dated 18 October 2022.

Regulation 131E and auditor pathway

The NMP records procedures required by regulation 131E and states that an auditor is to review the NMP.

Relationship with the endorsed methodology

What the NMP says about the NCTP

The NMP states that the endorsed Noise Compliance Testing Plan provides the complete operational measurement procedure and that, where a conflict exists, the NCTP takes precedence. It also identifies the Marshall Day Acoustics NCTP and Background Noise Monitoring Report as the documents from which operational limits were derived.

Stated source documents

The extract identifies the Marshall Day NCTP and Background Noise Monitoring Report. The later SLR K15aa Baseline Report dated 16 February 2021 is not identified in this stated reference pathway.

NCTP precedence and alternative locations

The NMP states that the NCTP takes precedence where a conflict exists and records that no alternative measurement points were identified at the time of writing.

The approved NCTP identifies six preferred locations, including the Yendon receivers K34aa, M29aa and N31ab.

October 2022 NMP

The NMP identifies H18aa, K15aa and L18aa as the three preferred measurement locations.

Background-noise and reporting procedures

Further technical extracts

These extracts show how the October 2022 NMP describes background-noise adjustment and reporting. They are presented as statements made by the NMP, not as independent repository findings.

Adjustment for background noise

The NMP states that pre-operational background-noise data is to be used to adjust compliance-monitoring results.

Operational noise reporting

The NMP states that compliance reports are to be submitted to EPA within six weeks of completing each stage of monitoring.
