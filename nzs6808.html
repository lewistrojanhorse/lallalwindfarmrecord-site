NZS 6808 Mandatory Definitions

Background sound, notional boundary, noise sensitive location and Special Audible Characteristics explained for non-technical readers.

Technical framework

Mandatory definitions shape the whole record

NZS 6808:2010 is treated in the record as the governing technical standard incorporated into the planning framework. The website presents the relevant concepts in plain language so readers can understand why the later documents matter.

B

Background sound level

The pre-existing sound environment against which wind farm noise limits and later assessment are understood.

N

Notional boundary

The boundary concept used to identify where assessment is made relative to a dwelling or other noise sensitive location.

S

Special Audible Characteristics

Recognisable sound characteristics such as tonality, impulsiveness or modulation that may affect assessment.

Definitions and record relevance

ConceptPlain-English meaningWhy it matters in this repository
Background Sound LevelThe sound level measured before wind turbines are operating in the area.Central to K15aa, noise limits and later compliance assessment.
Notional BoundaryA defined assessment boundary associated with a dwelling or noise sensitive building.Relevant to the relationship between monitoring position, dwelling location and compliance assessment.
Noise Sensitive LocationThe location where human amenity and noise impacts are assessed under the applicable framework.Relevant to J14aa, K15aa, receptor selection and measurement location records.
Special Audible CharacteristicsSound features that may make wind farm noise more noticeable or intrusive.Relevant to Annex B, attended observations, SAC identification and reporting.
ShallUsed in the record to identify mandatory requirements rather than discretionary recommendations.The Verification Schedule examines mandatory requirements identified from the governing framework.
Connection to verification

Definitions become verification questions

The Documentary Verification Schedule converts these technical concepts into documentary questions. It asks whether the disclosed record enables an independent reader to reconcile the governing definition with the monitoring, assessment and custody record.
