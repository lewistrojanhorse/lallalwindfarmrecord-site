Governing technical framework

NZS 6808:2010

Plain-English guide to how the wind farm noise Standard sits within the Lal Lal Wind Farm planning, testing and documentary verification pathway.

NZS 6808 in the GlossaryFramework
How to read this page

The Standard is part of the governing chain

This page does not reproduce NZS 6808:2010. It explains how the Standard is used in this repository: as the technical layer between the Planning Permit, the Noise Compliance Testing Plan, and later documentary verification.

Fact

NZS 6808:2010 provides the technical language used for wind farm sound assessment, including background sound, noise-sensitive locations and assessment methodology.

Why it matters

The permit and NCTP record pathway depends on how those technical concepts were applied, reported and later relied upon.

Evidence

The repository reads NZS 6808 through the Planning Permit, NCTP, K15aa record, SAC record, auditor material, FOI decisions and Verification Schedule.

Repository rule

Definitions are explained once in the Glossary

To avoid repeating technical definitions across the website, this page links first-use concepts to the Glossary and then returns readers to the evidence pages.

Background sound

The starting concept for background-dependent wind farm noise limits and the K15aa documentary pathway.

Noise-sensitive location

The receiver/location language used to understand where assessment and verification occur.

Special Audible Characteristics

The sound-character pathway connecting NZS concepts, attended observations and the SAC evidence page.
