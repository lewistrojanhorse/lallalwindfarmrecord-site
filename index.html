The question at the centre of this record

Do the available documents show that the Lal Lal Wind Farm's planning, technical and regulatory obligations are being met?

This website was created to help answer that question.

It shows the requirements, the evidence found, the gaps identified, the questions put to regulators and other organisations, and the responses received — or where no response was received.

Why this website was created

To show the gaps in the record

What the website does

The Lal Lal Wind Farm is governed by planning approvals, technical standards, noise requirements and regulatory duties.

The Trustee examined the available documents to see whether they explain how those obligations are being met.

Where the documents do not provide a clear answer, the website identifies the gap.

What happened when questions were asked

The website also shows:
the requests made to regulators and other organisations, the answers received, and where a question was not answered.

This allows readers to see the gap, the question asked about it, and the response — or lack of response — in the same record.

Administrative developments

Commissioning and DTP disclosure record

The following developments add primary source material to the public record. They record agency searches, decision notices and later clarification correspondence; they do not determine the legal or regulatory effect of the underlying events.

Moorabool Shire Council — planning administration and governance record

9–22 April 2026: the Trustee sought administrative clarification from Henry Bezuidenhout, Executive Manager Community Planning & Development, and from Governance concerning the Elaine meteorological monitoring facility, commissioning timing and the documentary basis of Council's historical administrative positions.

14 May 2026: Governance advised that the FOI processes were complete, its involvement had concluded and no further response would be provided.

20 July 2026: the Trustee issued a Notice to the Chief Executive Officer inviting Council to state whether its administrative record accurately reflects its historical administration of Planning Permit PL-SP/05/0461 while Council was identified as the Responsible Authority for administration and enforcement.

Open Council pathwayOpen 20 July Notice

DTP FOI CS019200 — decision and clarification

17 July 2026: DTP issued its Notice of Access Decision. The decision records eight documents located and states that no commissioning certificates, individual turbine commissioning records, practical completion certificates or handover certificates were located.

The Trustee subsequently sought administrative clarification concerning the identity of the applicant and the determination of Part 4 of the request. That clarification is not recorded as having been answered in the material presently published.

Open DTP decisionOpen Trustee clarification

Australian Energy Infrastructure Commissioner — documentary pathway

2025: the AEIC considered the Beneficiary's complaint and the Commissioner recorded reliance on EPA Victoria's stated regulatory position when concluding that the AEIC could not assist further.

March–July 2026: the Trustee provided the developing and crystallised administrative record to the AEIC for documentary completeness only, including the EPA reconciliation notice. No review or action was sought.

Open AEIC pathwayOpen Commissioner response
Present documentary position: the published record can identify the searches and decision outcomes recorded by Council and DTP, together with the Trustee's subsequent clarification correspondence. It does not infer from a “no documents found” outcome that a document never existed or determine the commissioning date by assumption.
Latest issued notices

Recent questions added to the record

These notices show the latest formal questions raised about identified documentary gaps.

Issued 20 July 2026

Notice — Administrative Record — Governance Consideration

Planning Permit PL-SP/05/0461 — Moorabool Shire Council

The Notice asks Council whether its administrative record accurately reflects its historical administration of the permit while Council was expressly identified as the Responsible Authority for administration and enforcement. It invites Council to identify any clarification, supplementation or correction it considers necessary.

The Notice expressly concerns historical permit administration. It does not concern present operational-noise regulation, EPA functions or a present-day compliance determination.

Read the NoticeOpen Council record
Issued 16 July 2026

Notice — Documentary Reconciliation of EPA's Current Regulatory Position

Lal Lal Wind Farm — K15aa

This Notice asks EPA Victoria to identify the documents that explain how its current position is reconciled with the laws, planning requirements, NZS 6808:2010 and other documents applying to K15aa.

It records a request for a documentary explanation. It does not make a compliance finding.

Read the NoticeOpen the K15aa page
Issued 29 June 2026

Notice — Crystallisation of Administrative and Regulatory Record

This Notice records the completion of the present phase of the Trustee's administrative and documentary record concerning the Lal Lal Wind Farm.

It explains that the documents assembled to that date were preserved as a stable public record while later developments could be added separately.

How the website is written

Plain English, backed by the documents

The website uses plain English wherever possible. Exact legal or technical wording is used only when it needs to be preserved.

The documents are published so readers can check the evidence, see the gaps and read the responses for themselves.
