Documentary issues

Key Documentary Issues

This page identifies the principal issues arising from comparison of the governing planning and technical requirements with the presently available reports, correspondence, verification material, Freedom of Information disclosures and agency responses.

Verification MethodGlossary
Reading position: An issue appears on this page because the available record raises a material documentary question. Inclusion does not itself establish non-compliance, breach, liability or a regulatory finding.
Documentary method

How an issue is identified

Each issue is examined through the same sequence so that the documentary test remains consistent across the record.

1

Governing requirement

Identify the permit condition, incorporated standard, endorsed plan, reporting obligation or statutory provision that supplies the relevant requirement.

2

Available evidence

Identify the reports, monitoring records, correspondence, audit material, decisions and disclosures that address that requirement.

3

Current documentary position

State what the record establishes, what instrument or explanation has not been identified, what question was raised and what response was received.

Reading discipline

What the issue pages distinguish

Careful use of the record requires different documentary propositions to remain separate.

A

What the document says

The page identifies the express wording, date, author, status and stated purpose of the source document.

B

What has not been identified

Where no instrument, approval, report or explanation has been located in the available record, the page states that limited documentary position.

C

What remains for a decision-maker

The record does not convert an evidentiary gap into a legal or regulatory finding. That distinction is preserved throughout.

Important: “Not identified in the available record” does not mean that a document could not exist elsewhere. It means the presently available source material does not identify it.
